problem statement

Young, innocent, brilliant and vibrant minds at the public secondary school level in most African countries lack the much-needed supports for continued performance. The power of academic excellence is less talked about in the society and students do not see reasons to improve on academic knowledge. More clearly, students spend most of their valuable times on various social media handles such as Facebook and Twitter, which have polarized the society with lots of negative vibes and thus further exacerbate the lack of interest for academic excellence. Additionally, secondary school curriculum lacks synopses that integrate academic knowledge with innovative thinking and problem-solving skills. Also, the tasks of nation building are fully shouldered by the government and hence, there is a lack of youth involvement in community development and they are less informed of the agenda of the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). These problems have increased the uncertainties associated with youth development in Africa as well as raised the question of whether the future African leaders are ready for the tasks of continental development or potential contributors of solutions to global grand challenges such as Food Security (SDGs 1 and 2), Climate Change (SDGs 7 and 13) and Pandemics (SDG 3).

on the inside

At Youth Mentors Hub, we are What Next Mentors and networks of mentors in the areas of graduate school preparations, work readiness, emotional intelligence, and entrepreneurship. Our Associates are supported on writing materials such as exercise books and texbooks. They are also being mentored on leadership and problem-solving skills , for instance, leveraging Mathematics problems at the high school level. Our Ambassadors who are undergraduates are mentored on community development strategies and skills needed to transition from the university to the workplace or graduate school.
